# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Creators and influencers who need a professional link-in-bio landing page, want to shorten URLs with their own brand, and rely on analytics to understand their audience. They come from platforms like Instagram, TikTok, YouTube, and Twitter/X. They want something that looks premium, works instantly, and makes them look professional without technical effort.

## Product Purpose

LinkNest replaces the fragmented toolkit of Linktree, Bitly, and QR code generators with one beautiful, self-hosted platform. Shorten URLs, create bio pages, generate QR codes, and track every click — all from a single dashboard. Success means creators never bounce between five tabs to manage their links.

## Brand Personality

Confident, warm, precise. Like Notion meets Linear — there's a polished editorial confidence, not startup bravado. The tone is direct and helpful, not hype-driven. Visuals are warm without being soft, precise without being cold. Every pixel earns its place.

## Anti-references

Generic SaaS templates. No Linktree or Bitly clones. No Inter font, no purple-to-blue gradients, no cards nested inside cards, no uppercase tracked eyebrows above every section, no "hero-metric" stat blocks. Nothing that looks like an off-the-shelf template.

## Design Principles

1. **Own the surface.** Every page, every state — empty, error, loading, edge case — is designed, not left to defaults. The dashboard should feel complete even with zero links.

2. **Confidence through restraint.** Fewer elements, more intention. Avoid decorative clutter (gradient text, glassmorphism as default, random particle backgrounds). Let the typography and spacing do the work.

3. **Warmth is carried by accent and typography, not by background tint.** The terracotta palette and serif typeface provide warmth. The body background stays neutral and true.

4. **Respect the creator's POV.** The interface is a tool, not a showcase. Every screen should get out of the way and put the creator's content — links, analytics, bio pages — front and center.

5. **Precision in every state.** Loaders, empty states, form validation, hover transitions, focus rings — none are afterthoughts. The polish of these moments defines product quality.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Standard best practices: WCAG AA contrast ratios (4.5:1 body, 3:1 large text), full keyboard navigation, focus indicators, reduced motion support via `prefers-reduced-motion`. No formal certification target, but no skipped affordances.
